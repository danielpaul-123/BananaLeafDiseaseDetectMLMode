Banana Insect Pest Diseases


Borers: Rhizome Weevil (Cosmopolites sordidus)

  • Symptoms of Damage: Grubs bore into the rhizome, leading to plant death. Dark tunnels appear in the rhizomes, causing withering of outer leaves and death of unopened pipes.
  • Identification: Eggs are white, laid on the upper part of the rhizome. Grubs are yellowish-white with red heads, while adults are dark brown to black weevils.
  • Management:
    • Use healthy, pest-free suckers for planting.
    • Trim the rhizome and maintain clean cultivation practices.
    • Remove pseudo stems below ground level and avoid susceptible varieties like Robusta and Karpooruvally.
    • Employ traps such as cosmolure or banana corm split traps to capture adult weevils.
    • Apply insecticides like carbofuran or phorate at the time of planting.

Pseudostem Borer (Odoiporus longicollis)

  • Symptoms of Damage: Grubs bore into the pseudostem, creating tunnels, which result in wilting and decomposition. Exudation of plant sap and blackened mass coming out from bore holes are early signs.
  • Identification: Eggs are yellowish-white, cylindrical, and laid randomly on the cut ends of pseudostem. Grubs are creamy white with dark heads, and adults are robust weevils.
  • Management:
    • Prune side suckers and remove dried leaves regularly.
    • Destroy infested plants by uprooting and burning.
    • Set pseudostem traps to reduce adult populations.
    • Apply dimethoate or other recommended insecticides for severe infestations.

Sap Feeders: Banana Aphid (Pentalonia nigronervosa)

  • Symptoms of Damage: Aphids cause stunted growth and bunchy top disease, with leaves forming a rosette-like appearance. Infestation prevents bunch production.
  • Identification: Nymphs are dark-colored, and adults have brownish bodies with black-veined wings.
  • Management:
    • Maintain clean cultivation and use pest-free suckers.
    • Destroy diseased plants along with the rhizome to control spread.
    • Encourage natural predators like ladybird beetles and lacewings.
    • Use biocontrol agents like Beauveria bassiana to manage aphid populations.

Leaf Feeders: Castor Hairy Caterpillar (Pericallia ricini)

  • Symptoms of Damage: Caterpillars scrape chlorophyll from leaves, leading to windowing, which affects unfurled leaves.
  • Identification: Larvae are black with brown heads and long hairs, while adults are grey-colored moths with dark spots on their pinkish wings.
  • Management:
    • Collect and destroy egg masses and caterpillars manually.
    • Use light traps to capture adult moths.
    • Spray insecticides like chlorpyrifos or quinalphos for severe infestations.

General Prevention and Management Strategies

  • Clean Cultivation: Ensure regular removal of dried and infested plant materials to minimize pest harboring areas.
  • Pest-Free Planting Material: Always use certified pest-free planting materials, such as tissue cultures or healthy suckers.
  • Natural Predators: Promote natural predators like ladybird beetles, lacewings, and parasitoid wasps to manage pest populations organically.
  • Crop Rotation: Avoid replanting banana in the same field consecutively to reduce soil-borne pests.
