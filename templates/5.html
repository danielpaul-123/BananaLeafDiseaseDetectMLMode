Banana Panama Disease (Fusarium Wilt)


What is Panama Disease?

  • Panama disease, also known as Fusarium wilt, is caused by the soil-borne fungus Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. cubense.
  • The disease affects bananas and is considered one of the most destructive banana diseases worldwide.
  • There are four races of the fungus, with Race 4 (Tropical and Subtropical) being the most serious threat to the banana industry, especially for Cavendish bananas.

What Are the Symptoms?

  • External Symptoms:
    • Yellowing of leaf margins on older leaves, followed by browning and collapse, forming a skirt of dead leaves around the plant base.
    • The heart leaves may remain upright, giving a spiky appearance to the plant.
    • As the disease progresses, the plant may appear wilted, and the pseudostem may split.
  • Internal Symptoms:
    • Discoloration of the vascular tissue in the corm and pseudostem, appearing as reddish-brown or black streaks or rings.
    • Infected plants rarely produce marketable bunches of bananas.

What Can Panama Disease Be Confused With?

  • In its early stages, Panama disease can be mistaken for nutritional deficiencies or water stress.
  • It can also be confused with bacterial wilts like Moko or blood disease, but these diseases produce bacterial ooze and affect fruit, unlike Panama disease.

How Is Panama Disease Spread?

  • Spread occurs through infected planting material, root-to-root contact, or contaminated soil, water, and tools.
  • The fungus can persist in the soil for many years, making it difficult to eliminate once established.
  • Movement of soil on vehicles, machinery, or shoes can also spread the disease.

Where Is Panama Disease Found?

  • Panama disease Race 1 and Race 2 are under quarantine in banana-producing areas of Queensland, New South Wales, and Western Australia.
  • Race 4 (both Tropical and Subtropical strains) is under strict quarantine management in the Northern Territory and parts of Queensland.

Prevention and Control Strategies

  • Exclusion and Quarantine:
    • Use only clean, certified planting material, such as tissue-cultured plants.
    • Avoid introducing infected soil or plant material to healthy areas, and follow strict quarantine regulations.
  • Farm Hygiene:
    • Disinfect machinery, equipment, and footwear before entering and leaving banana farms to prevent the spread of contaminated soil.
    • Avoid sharing farm tools or machinery between farms.
  • Early Detection and Reporting:
    • Regularly inspect crops for symptoms of Panama disease and report any signs immediately.
    • Do not attempt to remove or destroy affected plants yourself, as this can spread the disease further.
  • Destruction of Infected Plants:
    • Affected plants should be destroyed in situ using methods such as herbicide injection to minimize disturbance of infected soil.

How to Protect Your Farm from Panama Disease?

  • Ensure only pest-free planting material is used on your farm.
  • Maintain strict biosecurity measures to prevent the introduction of infected soil or plant material.
  • Wash and disinfect all machinery, equipment, and vehicles before entering the farm.
